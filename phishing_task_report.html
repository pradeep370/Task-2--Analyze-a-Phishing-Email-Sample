Analyze a Phishing Email Sample

Date: 05 August 2025

Submitted by: Pradeep S

Objective

To analyze a suspicious email and identify common phishing indicators such as fake links, spoofed sender, and social engineering tricks, helping to improve email threat detection skills.

Tools Used

  • Online Link Checkers
  • Email Header Analyzers (MX Toolbox)
  • VirusTotal (for link checking)

Analyzing the Phishing Email

  • Fake sender email address
  • Suspicious/mismatched link
  • Fake attachment request
  • Spelling or grammar errors

Red Flags Identified

  1. Generic Greeting: Uses “Dear Valued Customer” instead of a real name.
  2. Fake Sender Email: Sent from tomandcherry403@gmail.com instead of an official domain like @yourbank.com.
  3. Urgent and Threatening Language: Mentions suspension within 24 hours to create fear.
  4. Suspicious Link: Appears to be secure but is not a real bank domain: https://yourbank.secure-verify-login.net.
  5. Grammatical Issues: Unnatural and unprofessional wording.
  6. Fake Attachment: Includes a PDF asking for sensitive data like ATM PIN and login credentials.
